The job

The Emerald's president is responsible for all the company's business affairs and reports to the board of directors. The president directly manages the advertising and business departments and The Garage, the company's new ventures division.

The president supervises five professional staff members: Advertising director, business manager, mobile/web developer and two part-time creative/ad designers.

The president is expected to work with Emerald students, staff and partners to deliver on three major priorities:

Leadership: Craft the company's vision and strategy

Finances: Manage all aspects the company's operating budget

Business development: Create new ventures that grow our revenue, readership and relevance as a media company. Our existing ventures include events, photo booth, creative ad agency, housing search web app and more.

A note about the newsroom: The Emerald's student editor in chief is responsible for all the company's editorial content. The president has no authority or responsibility for the editorial content of the Emerald's news products.

The editor in chief is hired by the board of directors and is a peer of the president on the organizational chart. The president and editor in chief operate as a team to run the company.

The Emerald

The Emerald is an independent nonprofit media company. We have served the University of Oregon with news and information since 1900. Our mission is to train students, serve the UO community and run a financially sustainable nonprofit business.

The Emerald produces a news magazine in print twice a week and online news 24/7. But we do a bunch of other stuff, too: Marketing, events, advertising and tech ventures. Our students work for The Oregonian, The New York Times, Wall Street Journal, The Chicago Tribune, Nike, GQ, Google, Facebook, Amazon.

In 2013, the Emerald's Revolution transitioned the company from a traditional newspaper to a modern college media company. College Media Matters, the leading college newspaper blog, named the Emerald the 2013-14 college newspaper of the year. Our Revolution has also been covered by Oregon Quarterly, Huffington Post, Harvard's Nieman Journalism Lab, Bloomberg Businessweek, USA Today College and industry analysts and groups, including PBS MediaShift, Poynter, Ken Doctor and Steve Buttry. Since 1997, the Emerald has earned the Pulitzer Prize of college journalism — the Associated Collegiate Press' Pacemaker award — three times and has been a finalist in print and digital categories five times. The Oregon way

We think different here.

The state tagline used to be: "We love dreamers."

Iconic former Gov. Tom McCall — an Emerald alum — made Oregon a national leader in the 1970s environmental movement with the bottle bill, beach bill and land-use laws.

Nike founder Phil Knight — also an Emerald alum — revolutionized shoes, sports and marketing.

In 1966. Emerald managing editor Annette Buchanan refused to disclose her sources to the local district attorney. Her fight led the Legislature to create Oregon's first Shield Law.

Former Emerald editor Paul Brainerd grew up to be the "father of desktop publishing," according to The New York Times. Then there's the University of Oregon's football program: Fast, fashionable and, often, first.

Oregon is home for pioneers. If you're one, too, this can be your next home.
